About Me

I want to become a game developer.

Last year I decided that I would finally learn to code, something I had always wanted to do. I started learning the basics of web development focusing on HTML, CSS, and JS. I found a lot of amazing resources (see: Scrimba) but I also found more "motivational" content.

This content would have titles like "How I learned to code in 3 months" or "How I would learn if I had to start over".

While I imagine that the makers of this content had good intentions, it always struck me as simplistic, surface level, and unrealistic. Most people don't have the time or resources to code 8 hours a day.

I decided that if I was ever in the position to learn something brand new I would document it from beginning so if anyone else wanted to know what it was like to learn that new thing, they could follow my real journey.

So here we are, at the beginning of something new, my journey towards learning game development.

I will be honest, game development never seemed like anything I would be interested in. Don't get me wrong, I love video games, but up until this point, video games were just a hobby.

Last year, I started learning more about the world of VR and specifically, VR Chat. VR Chat was giving people of all backgrounds the opportunity meet new people, be their authentic selves, and live in a world free from the barriers they faced in their "real lives".

Up until this point, I had really only focused on mobile and web applications in the fitness space as a Product Manager. VR seemed, at best, a fad to me. (It probably did not help that my one experience with VR was 10 years ago when I put on a giant "VR" helmet and felt like I was going to vomit).

But, when I saw what this technology could do for other people, I saw its potential.

And now, I am learning game development to build in the VR space.

Why This Is Unique

Let's be honest, I am not the first person to document learning something new. But, I think by following my journey, you can take something away, at least, I hope so.

So, what makes this a challenge or worth reading/following? What makes this different?

1. I have a full time job. For ~8 hours a day, I am working in IT at a local company where I live, so no unrealistic time commitments. Furthermore, I run every day in the morning and box three days a week in the evening, so my free time is even more limited.

2. This is time-boxed. A year sounds short, but it really isn't. I learned web development in a year, so why not game development?

3. I am no one special. I don't have excess time or resources. I work in technology, but even that isn't as unique as you might think. I just happen to stare at a computer more than most people.

Who knows, maybe this will be a total, overwhelming disaster.

Whatever happens, I will document it here for you to read.
