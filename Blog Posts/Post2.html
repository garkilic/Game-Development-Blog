Silly Mistakes

Date: Sept. 11, 2022

Coding is a balance of love and hate, genius and idiotic, simple-minded and prophetic. Some days when I am building something it all seems to click. Everything I write seems to work and all of my practice and knowledge seems to click into place. Other days I cannot center a div or fix a simple error. This week I had a mistake that is a little embarrassing to write about.

Let me explain this lapse in judgement so you can avoid this same mistake.

When it comes to text editors, there are many options, but for a lot of people Visual Studio Code is the obvious choice. It is relatively simple, maintained by Microsoft, free, and has a ton of useful extensions for cleaner and better code.

When you start working in Unity you will eventually have to get into scripting (just as I did this week) and you will have to choose a text-editor to work within when writing C# or Javascript for your game. I, of course, used Visual Studio Code because it's the text editor I use all the time.

I noticed something was off immediately. I was writing C# (something I just started) but the Unity Intellisense was not working. "Intellisense" is basically auto-complete functionality specific to Unity/Game Development practices. It's incredibly helpful in this instance because there are a ton of Unity-specific components and functions you may not know off the top of your head but are incredibly important. It also helps because C# is incredibly literal. If you miss on "()" or ";" or capital letter, your game will not work.

All of this to say that Intellisense is important.

I kid you not when I say that I spent hours trying to get this working. I watched Youtube videos, read forum posts about this specific issue, downloaded extensions, I did everything I could think of. I could not figure it out. I was lost and figured that this was just not going to be available for my journey.

Yesterday, I decided, "fuck it" I am going to solve this, and on the first video I watched it clicked and my brain broke.

You see, there is Visual Studio Code, which I and a ton of other developers use, but, for some unknown reason there is also Visual Studio, a completely different text editor used for more complex applications, also maintained by Microsoft with a frustratingly similar logo.

That's right, I was using the wrong editor, for a week, trying to solve this impossible problem.

I felt so dumb that I questioned not only my coding abilities but my sanity.

At the end of the day it's not a huge deal, I figured it out, and I was happy that I did because Intellisense now works. But, as you go on this adventure, especially if you aren't a professional developer, learn to be okay with the fact that days you will feel like a genius and other days you will feel like a monkey trying to write Shakespeare.

If you want a book that covers these feelings and the general life and feelings of a programmer I would suggest a book called 'Coders' by Clive Thompson. He perfectly captures this feeling because it's universal in this field.

One more story before I finish this post. Yesterday I was playing video games with a friend. He asked what I was working on and I sent him this site.

"What's this timer mean?" he asked.

"It's the amount of time I gave myself to learn this"

"Why are you wasting it here playing games with me?" he said.

...

Ah, the power of the countdown.

Until next week...
