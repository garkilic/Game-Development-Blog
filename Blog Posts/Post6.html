Comparisons and Social Media

Date: Oct. 10th, 2022

Some days I feel good, and some days I don't. Today is a good day.

I am on a road trip right now traveling up the Oregon Coast with my partner, Anna. We had been feeling stuck both mentally and physically in The Bay for awhile and we decided, rather quickly, to take a roadtrip to check out more of the West Coast that we had never seen.

It seemed that right as we got on the 1 and I started seeing trees I felt a weight lifted off of me. I think when you are stuck and have anxiety, it can be easy to feel like everything is so pressing and important. It may be universal, but especially in the Bay Area it can feel like there is nothing more important than a career, then technology, then constantly wondering how you are ever going to afford a house.

This is partly due to the area. There are a lot of amazing things about living in Oakland, but there are an equal amount of issues, and this balance can lead to a love/hate relationship. It is also attributed to the culutre which seems to be emphasized by social media like LinkedIn.

I will admit something to you, I am a recovering "hustle culture" addict. I was not spouting nothingburgers to motivate you, but I definitley pushed myself harder than I should, and I will partly attribute that to the toxic productivity of the internet. I will also admit that I relapse sometimes. When I have free time I refresh my email and my LinkedIn hoping for... something. Maybe it's validation. Maybe it's the excitement of the potenital of something new (new job, new career, new person to talk to). This is not something I want to do, but I do it anyway.

I find it kind of crazy that I can know how these social media systems work and still I get sucked into it. I have blockers on my phone, my computer, and still, I fall down these rabbit-holes reading some awful content.

The truth is, I am a sucker for validation. I look at reviews for anything I purchase, for any meal or resteraunt I go to, and for any path (career or otherwise) that I take. I want to be reassured again and again that I am doing the right thing.

I don't want to be this way.

The reason I talk about these issues, is because right as I some some Redwoods and got to the ocean, I felt better. It reminded me that none of that stuff really matters. I felt more confident, more sure of myself than I had in a long time.

This made me realize that this is how I want be all the time. Not that it's entirely possible for me to move to the Redwoods (although that is kind of the plan eventually), but I can take steps to get away from the constant validation.

Here's the plan:

  • No more LinkedIn or HackerNews. It will be super-blocked on all of my devices.
  • No more looking up reviews. Just try it out and see what happens.
  • Avoid any career-related content. I love what I am doing with my development journey and I know it's the right path. I don't need anyone else confirming or disagreeing with my path.
  • No more social media.
  • No more asking people what they thing (to a degree) and just making a decision.

This is me making a pledge to anyone reading this. I will be better.
