Staying Consistent

Date: Sept. 19th, 2022

I have always wished that I was the type of person who could pursue a goal doggedly. You know the type. The person that can just work and work obsessively. That's just not me.

Maybe this type of person doesn't really exist. Even if they did exist, I am sure it's not the way it is in the movies and books. You see, in movies, they tend to cut out all of the boring stuff that real life contains (imagine if they didn't, sheesh).

All of this to say, don't beat yourself up (like I do (and shouldn't)) regarding working hard enough. I want to take the approach that any progress is good regardless if you can spend an hour or ten hours a week working on this.

So what was accomplished in the last week?

Well, I finished my first simple game. I followed along on the Brackeys tutorial (posted on this site in the resources section, and I gotta tell you, it was a great feeling.

One of the reasons why I love this type of development so much is because there is immediate feedback and it's so literal. As a web developer, you can write code, and it may work but it may not work well, there are hacky things you can do, but Javascript is finiky and web development can sometimes (most of the time) make me want to bang my head against a wall.

When it comes to game development though, you already have a game engine (Unity) so you have a lot already to work with. Put on top of that C# (which is what I use, but you could use JS if you are a masochist) which is just fails even if you are missing a ; or a capital letter, it's awesome.

Honestly, that's part of the reason why I loved math so much. Either it works or it doesn't, it's a 0 or a 1. Love it or hate it, either the answer is right or wrong. (Don't come at me math people, the metaphor doesn't extend very far, whatever).

What I am trying to say is that I made my first game and game development is fun.

Following game #1 will be game #2 which will also be following a tutorial (also linked in Resources), after that I will be on my own and building my game from scratch.

This type of learning isn't for everyone, but I know what helps me is an introduction to a subject with a lot of small wins. After those small wins I throw myself into the deep end and figure it out (with the bit of knowledge I gained). Then I will Google and study until I complete whatever the project is.

Next week, I will present my first game idea in more detail so I have a repository of my ideas (and to fill you in). But for now, this is the update.

Keep it up and we will talk again soon.

-Griffin
